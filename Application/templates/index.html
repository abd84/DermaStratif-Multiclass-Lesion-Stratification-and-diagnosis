Skin Disease Information

  • Melanoma: A dangerous type of skin cancer originating in melanocytes. Severity: High Melanoma is the most dangerous form of skin cancer and can spread to other parts of the body if not detected early. Early diagnosis and treatment are critical.
  • Melanocytic Nevus: Commonly known as a mole, usually benign. Severity: Low Generally harmless, but some moles can develop into melanoma. Any changes in size, shape, or color should be monitored.
  • Basal Cell Carcinoma (BCC): The most common skin cancer, rarely spreads. Severity: Moderate It grows slowly and rarely spreads but can cause significant local damage if left untreated. Often associated with sun exposure.
  • Actinic Keratosis: Precancerous scaly patches caused by sun exposure. Severity: Moderate to High If untreated, it can develop into squamous cell carcinoma. Early treatment is recommended.
  • Benign Keratosis: Non-cancerous skin growths. Severity: Low These growths are harmless and do not require treatment unless they become bothersome.
  • Dermatofibroma: Benign skin growth often caused by minor injuries. Severity: Low Completely harmless and rarely requires treatment unless it causes discomfort.
  • Vascular Lesion: Marks caused by abnormal blood vessels. Severity: Low to Moderate Mostly benign but might require treatment for cosmetic reasons or if bleeding occurs.
  • Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC): A type of skin cancer that can spread. Severity: High More aggressive than basal cell carcinoma. It can grow and spread to other parts of the body if untreated, making early detection important.
